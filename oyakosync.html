OyaKoSync

A lightweight multimodal framework for contextualized measurement of mother-infant interaction

OyaKoSync is a multimodal app designed to record and visualize natural mother-infant interaction using synchronized video, audio, physiology-related measures, and emotion/context logs.

Mother-infant interaction Multimodal sensing Behavior analysis Affective computing Physiological signals iOS app Japanese / English

Overview

OyaKoSync was developed to support naturalistic observation of mother-infant interaction beyond structured laboratory settings. The system synchronizes front and back camera recording, audio capture, behavioral feature extraction, physiology-related signals, and emotion/context logging within a unified review framework.

The project aims to support richer interpretation of dyadic interaction by linking interaction-episode dynamics with broader daily emotional, physiological, and activity context.

Key Features

• Synchronized front and back camera recording

• Simultaneous audio capture

• Behavioral signal extraction from facial and body movement

• Affect-related timeline visualization

• Physiology-related data integration

• Post-episode emotion and context logging

• Synchronized playback and multimodal review
