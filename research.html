OyaKoSync

A lightweight multimodal framework for contextualized measurement of mother-infant interaction

OyaKoSync is a lightweight multimodal measurement system designed to record and visualize natural mother-infant interaction in everyday settings. Using smartphone-based synchronized front and back camera capture, audio recording, physiology-related measures, and emotion/context logs, the system supports temporally aligned review of interaction dynamics across multiple modalities.

The project was developed to help extend parent-infant interaction research beyond structured laboratory paradigms into more naturalistic environments. It supports multimodal observation of facial behavior, body movement, vocal activity, affect-related estimates, and contextual logging within a unified review framework.

The broader goal of OyaKoSync is to support contextualized interpretation of dyadic interaction by linking rich interaction-episode recording with lower-burden monitoring of daily emotional, physiological, and activity context.
